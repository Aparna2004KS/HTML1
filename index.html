BIRDS KINGDOM


PEACOCK

The peacock, scientifically known as Pavo cristatus, is a species of bird renowned for its vibrant and elaborate plumage, particularly the male's (peacock) long, iridescent tail feathers. It is the national bird of India and is deeply rooted in Indian culture and mythology.Peacocks are special due to their vibrant and elaborate plumage, particularly the long, iridescent tail feathers of the male (peacock) which are used in elaborate mating displays. They are also known for their unique behaviors, including ground nesting, territory defense, and the ability to survive in diverse habitats.


PIGEON

A pigeon is a bird belonging to the family Columbidae, which also includes doves. These birds are known for their stout bodies, small heads, and short, slender bills. Pigeons are found worldwide and are known for their diverse colors and patterns. PARROT

Parrots are vibrant, intelligent birds known for their colorful plumage, strong, hooked beaks, and ability to mimic sounds, including human speech.Most of the time parrots are not easy to catch. Even after being pets, they have a bad mannerism if not maintained properly. Some researchers state that most wild birds can be controlled by banding or wing tagging, but parrots are not birds to be easily trifled with; they chew off such affections. The behaviour of parrots differs from type to type. Some of them are strong and have direct flight whereas most of the species spend their time perching or climbing. Parrots sometimes walk in a rolling gait manner too.


EAGLE

Eagles are large, powerful birds of prey known for their sharp talons, hooked beaks, and exceptional eyesight. They are typically found worldwide, inhabiting diverse environments from forests to coastal areas. Eagles are skilled hunters, relying on their strong wings for soaring and powerful legs with sharp talons for catching prey.Its full-grown wingspan can reach a full eight feet in the biggest birds which also allows them to soar on the updrafts of wind to as high as 20,000 feet above the earth. (That's over 3 ¾ miles!) Eagles, as a species, are also the only birds that intentionally fly above storm clouds to avoid the rain.


WOODPECKER

The term "wood parker bird" likely refers to a woodpecker, a family of birds known for their characteristic drilling and pecking into trees. They have strong beaks for this purpose and long, sticky tongues to extract insects. Many woodpecker species are found in India, including the Indian Woodpecker and the Greater Yellownape.Woodpeckers have sharp and strong beaks for effective drilling into the trunk. The long sticky tongue helps them grasp insects. To prevent brain damage due to repeated drilling and pecking, Woodpeckers have certain features. Woodpeckers have a flexible and small brain.

Doves are often used as a symbol of peace.Unlike other birds, doves don't tuck their heads in their wings when they sleep. Though they breed with different doves during breeding season, doves mate for life with one companion bird. The pigeon beats its wings up to ten times per second, and can go 16 hours without rest.


DUCK


Ducks are a group of species of water birds - relatively small in size and with shorter necks than their close cousins of swans and geese. Along with their longer necked cousins, they make up the biological family of Anatidae.They are capable of abstract thinking and have deep emotional lives. Ducklings can tell the difference between objects based on their color or shape, demonstrating abstract thought capabilities. They also show affection for their siblings, mothers, and paddling-mates and, like all animals, are capable of feeling pain.


SWAN

Swans are special for their majestic appearance, graceful swimming, and unique behaviors like lifelong pair bonds and strong territoriality during breeding season. They are also among the largest flying birds, known for their elegant long necks and powerful wings. Swans are large, elegant, and graceful waterfowl characterized by their long necks and predominantly white plumage, though some species like the black swan have black feathers. They are known for their majestic swimming and powerful flight with necks outstretched. Swans are found in temperate regions of both the Northern and Southern Hemispheres, inhabiting various bodies of water like lakes, rivers, and marshes.


SPARROW

Sparrows are small, plump, and typically brown or grey birds with short tails and stout, conical beaks. They are social birds, often found in flocks, and are known for their ability to adapt to various environments, including urban areas. Males and females often exhibit distinct plumage differences, particularly in species like the House Sparrow. House Sparrows are sociable birds and often live in small groups, close to people. In fact, they've lived alongside humans for centuries, taking advantage of spilt grain and food scraps.


KINGFISHER

Kingfishers are known for their vibrant colors, distinctive hunting style, and unique adaptations for diving and catching fish. They are skilled underwater hunters, using their sharp beaks and keen vision to spot and capture prey. Kingfishers also possess a nictitating membrane that protects their eyes underwater, and their beaks are aerodynamically designed for efficient dives. The New Zealand native Kōtare / Kingfisher is a distinctive bird with stunning plumage and feathers that glint iridescent green blue. Kingfishers are symbolic of freedom, courage, adventure, balance, and free spirit.
